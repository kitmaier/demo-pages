The Puzzle

This page is based on a video about a logic puzzle.

There is a solution of this puzzle with at most 5 questions in the worst case.

Without loss of generality, because all the names and codewords start out interchangeable, we may as well assume that the first question is asked of the first person and the first response is Foo.

We will need to find out the identity of all 4 people and which codeword means Maybe, but we will not need to know and will not find out the meaning of the other two codewords.

We will start by listing out all of the possible combinations, or parallel worlds, which could be consistent with that first question.

Then we will list out all of the answers to the questions. In many cases, these answers are totally determined by the identity of the person the question is asked of. In the other cases, the answers can be freely demanded by phrasing the question appropriately.

We will require a notion of trustworthiness, where the most trustworthy are the Mathematician and Physicist, the next most trustworthy is the Philosopher, and the least trustworthy is the Engineer.

Q1 to Alice: Which of the other three people is the most trustworthy?

Foo: Bob

Bar: Carol

Baz: Dave

Q2 to Bob: Which of the other three people is the most trustworthy?

Foo: Alice

Bar: Carol

Baz: Dave

Q3 if Q2 answer was Bar: Which of the other three people is the most trustworthy?

Foo: Alice

Bar: Bob

Baz: Dave

Q3 if Q2 answer was Baz: Which of the other three people is the most trustworthy?

Foo: Alice

Baz: Bob

Bar: Carol
